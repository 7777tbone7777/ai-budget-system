# Additional U.S. State Tax Incentive Research

Research conducted: 2025-11-20
States researched: 30 missing states from original PDF extraction

## States with Active Programs

### New York (üî• Major Program)
- **Incentive**: 30-40% Tax Credit
- **Type**: Refundable/Transferable
- **Annual Cap**: $800M (expanded in 2025, highest in state history)
- **Base Credit**: 30% on qualified production expenses
- **Uplifts**:
  - 10% on qualified labor in certain upstate counties
  - 10% "Production Plus" bonus for studios with $100M+ spend
  - 5% bonus for qualifying independent projects
- **Independent Film Pool**: $100M earmarked
- **Minimum Spend**: $500K
- **Key Features**: Streamlined payment timeline (2025+)

### North Carolina
- **Incentive**: 25% Rebate
- **Type**: Grant (changed from tax credit in 2014)
- **Annual Cap**: $31M
- **Project Caps**:
  - TV Series: $15M
  - Feature Films: $7M
  - Commercials: $250K
- **Minimum Spend**:
  - Features: $1.5M
  - TV/Streaming Movies: $500K
  - TV Series: $500K per episode average
  - Commercials: $250K
- **Compensation Cap**: $1M per person
- **Requirements**: 75% of funding secured before applying
- **2024 Performance**: $302M in direct spending

### Massachusetts (üî• Major Program)
- **Incentive**: 25% Production Credit + 25% Payroll Credit
- **Type**: Transferable Tax Credit
- **Annual Cap**: None
- **Minimum Spend**: $50K
- **Key Features**:
  - Sales tax exemption
  - No residency requirements
  - No project caps
  - Credits can be cashed out at 90% with state
  - Can transfer at market rate
- **Requirements**: 75% of budget or filming in-state for full benefits

### Arizona (New Program - 2022)
- **Incentive**: 15-22.5% Tax Credit
- **Type**: Tax Credit
- **Annual Cap**: $100M (75% for Qualified Production Facilities, 25% for Practical Locations)
- **Tiered Structure**:
  - Under $10M: 15% base (17.5% BTL resident labor)
  - $10M-$35M: 17.5% base (20% BTL resident labor)
  - Over $35M: 20% base (22.5% BTL resident labor)
- **Uplifts**:
  - 2.5% for resident BTL labor
  - 2.5% for using qualified facility + in-state post
- **Sunset**: December 31, 2043

### Kentucky
- **Incentive**: 30-35% Refundable Tax Credit
- **Type**: Refundable
- **Annual Cap**: $75M
- **Rates**:
  - 30% on qualified expenditures and non-resident payroll
  - 35% on resident payroll
  - 5% uplift for Enhanced Incentive Counties
- **Minimum Spend**:
  - Features/TV/Industrial: $250K
  - Documentaries/Broadway: $20K
- **Performance**: 250+ productions, 27,000 jobs since 2022

### Tennessee
- **Incentive**: Up to 25% Grant
- **Type**: Grant
- **Annual Allocation**: $8.5M (FY 2024)
- **Benefits**:
  - Occupancy sales tax rebate after 90 continuous hotel days
  - No state income taxes on wages
- **Requirements**: On Qualified Tennessee Expenditures

### Texas
- **Incentive**: Up to 31% Cash Grant (with stackable bonuses)
- **Type**: Cash Grant (Texas Moving Image Industry Incentive Program - TMIIIP)
- **Base Rate**: Varies
- **Bonuses** (2.5% each, stackable):
  - Filming in Underutilized/Economically Distressed Areas
  - 5% veterans in cast/crew
  - 10% spend on Texas in-state post-production
- **Requirements**:
  - 60% of filming days in Texas
  - 55% of cast/crew must be residents

### Florida (‚ö†Ô∏è No State Program)
- **State Program**: NONE (ended ~10 years ago)
- **County Programs**:
  - **Miami-Dade** (Largest): Up to 20% cash rebate, $50M over 5 years, $5M minimum spend
  - **Broward**: 20-30% rebates (largest local fund in state)
  - **Pinellas**: 15-30% cash rebate
  - **Duval**: 15-20% rebates
  - **Hillsborough (Tampa)**: Up to 10% marketing grant (max $150K per project)

## States with NO Active Programs

### Michigan
- **Status**: NO CURRENT PROGRAM (ended 2015)
- **Proposed**: Multimedia Jobs Act (2024) - in legislative review
  - Would provide 30% for Michigan residents, 20% for nonresidents
  - 25-30% for shooting in Michigan
  - $125M annual cap (years 1-3), rising to $275M (years 7-10)
  - $300K minimum spend (20+ min projects), $50K (under 20 min)
  - $500K compensation cap per person
  - 10-year sunset
